About Me

I'm a Mendenhall Postdoctoral Fellow at the U.S. Geological Survey in Golden, CO. I am broadly interested in tectonic controls on seismic hazard. I investigate the physical mechanisms controlling earthquake nucleation, rupture, and arrest across a variety of depths, from shallow earthquakes to Earth’s deepest tremors. I explore Earth's interior across a variety of scales, from shallow crustal heterogeneities that impact hazard estimates to subduction zone systems that inform our understanding of plate tectonics.

I will be joining the Department of Earth Sciences at the University of Oregon as an Assistant Professor in December 2025. Please reach out if you're a student or post-doc interested in exploring our planet with seismology.
